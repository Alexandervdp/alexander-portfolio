Bachelor's Thesis: Biomechanical Ornithopter

As my final year Bachelor's project, my colleague (Tibo Opdebeeck) and I took on the challenge of biomechanically replicating a bird's flight. The result was an award-winning ornithopter (a robotic bird).

We developed multiple prototypes that simulated the complex wing flap as accurately as possible, combining mechanics, electronics, and aerodynamics to turn an idea into a working prototype. All components were custom-designed, 3D-printed, and lasercut by us. By combining knowledge from these different domains, we arrived at a functional and well-thought-out technological solution.

Innovation

Simulated biomechanics of a bird's wing flap using integrated mechanics, electronics, and aerodynamics.

Rapid Prototyping

All components were personally designed, 3D-printed, and lasercut to create multiple working prototypes.

Recognition
